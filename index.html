Our Story

Built in a Vermont barn since 2008

Grainwork started when our founder, Thomas Aldric, couldn't find a desk worth keeping. So he built one — in his grandfather's timber-frame barn with hand tools and a secondhand bandsaw.

Fifteen years later, we still work in that same barn. We've added three more craftspeople, a few more saws, and over 4,200 desks shipped across North America. The ethos hasn't changed: every desk should outlast its owner.

We choose our wood the way a tailor chooses cloth — for character, for grain, for the way it will look 30 years from now.

Our Story

Slow Craft in a Fast World

We started Grainwork in a 400 sq ft garage with one table saw and the belief that furniture should outlive its owner.

Every piece passes through the hands of a single craftsperson from rough lumber to final finish. We don't run assembly lines. We don't rush. We mill, plane, fit, and finish the way it's been done for generations — because that's the only way to get it right.

  • FSC-certified lumber from Oregon & Washington forests
  • Water-based, VOC-free finishes on every surface
  • Carbon-offset shipping on all domestic orders
  • White-glove in-home delivery & setup included
